Mapping Twitter
Interaction

Supporter numbers aren’t everything. There’s a complex geography to how Twitter communities interact, especially around an election. In the following community graph, each node is a tweep and each link is interactions (e.g. retweets). Here, the larger nodes represent accounts getting lots of attention - lots of likes, lots of retweets. Their color defines who they vote for, or at least who they tweet about the most.

People share lot of links via retweets. On the right hand panel are the links being shared most around by this community.

Media
Sentiment

Historically, the media has shaped how people think - and, conversely, turned out to be a good indicator of public opinion. We tap into Google News to find the most relevant news articles, analyze the sentiment therein, and map it. Left hand side shows how sentiments changed over time and on the right are the articles we’re looking at right now.
